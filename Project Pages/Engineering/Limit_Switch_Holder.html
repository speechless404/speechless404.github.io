Overview & Role

Creating a limit switch holder (LSH) or limit switch mount for the bottom of our airbrake. (The airbrake is a system that intentionally increases the drag of our rocket so that we can reach the desired altitude in the _____ compeitition.)

This was an individual task so all work was done by me.

Links to the files I created in this project can be found at the bottom of this page.

Version 1

With my first model I didnt know what I was doing and simply attatched the LSH to whatever was in the area. As I gained more experience I learned about tolerances, where screws could go, counterbores, leaving space for soldering/wiring and iterated my design along with the ever changing airbrake internal design.

The image shows my first model and my following models can be seen below.

Version 1
V2 - I understood a bit more but the design was still a little janky.
Testing my V2 design inside the assembly.
V3 - Changed to this from V2 for a better grip.
V4 - The internal assembly for the airbrake was changed an I needed to adapt accordingly.
V5 - Our internals changed again, but at the same time I reached a design that could fit our ever changing internals with little iteration.
V6 - Nearly identical to V5 just with holes in different places. I printed it out with an ULTIMAKER printer in PETG for testing.
Final design — The holes moved around a little again and this was the final model used in our rocket.
