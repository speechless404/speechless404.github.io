Overview & Role

My first self designed and manufactured project at UMD. The goal was to create an OTV capable of navigating to a "planting zone," deploying a seed, securing a soil sample, and returning to the starting point.

This was a team project and my role was to help design and construct the otv along with handle the manufacturing of all custom parts.

Links to the files I created in this project can be found at the bottom of this page.

Final OTV design — fully assembled and tested.

Sensor Mount

One of the things I was responsible for designing was the sensor mount. In our V1, we designed to have 2, one in front of each wheel, hense the slightly crooked design.

V1 — Printed and test fitted design.
V2 — improved design with better sensor placement and integration.

Timing Belt Pully System

One of my teammates wanted to do a timing belt pully system for the drive train with 4 wheel drive. We worked together to design the pullys and gear ratios. I then printed the unique shapes out of PLA, lasercut the flat drivetrain peices, and printed the timing belt out of TPU.

After a few iterations, we got it to work. The picture shows our final design of the timing belt pully system.

Final design of the timing belt pully system.

Arm

I also made the arm that would collect the soil sample. We tested a bunch of designs and found that tape actually worked best for the soil we needed to collect. So based off that, I made a design that could maximize surface area of the tape.

After a few iterations of length, spring width, and tape thickness, I got a design that could reliably collect soil.

Our final OTV design.
Us putting the OTV together.

Conclusion

Our OTV ended up not working as expected due to navigation issues which disallowed us to complete the individual tasks. In testing, our OTV could complete each task individually but when we put everything together, the navigation issues caused us to fail the overall mission.

Next time it seems like we need to ensure everything gets finished earlier so that there is more time to connect the dots. Or we could create a simple solution first then change things after we know ce can connect the parts.
