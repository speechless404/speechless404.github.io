Overview & Role

Originally tasked with creating a two-stage L1 rocket, I had to pivot to making a one-stage rocket first. Below you will find my work in simulation, CAD design, and manufacturing, as well as the iterations I went through to reach my final product.

This was my personal club project so all work was done by me.

Links to the files I created in this project can be found at the bottom of this page.

[ image ]

Simulation

I used OpenRocket to create the initial design for my two-stage rocket, then took the sustainer of that design and redesigned it as a dual-deploy L1 rocket.

The image shows my final sustainer design — also my L1 rocket. The design satisfies constraints on length, width, and stability.

OpenRocket simulation — final sustainer / L1 design.

CAD Design

I modelled all manufactured parts in SOLIDWORKS and assembled them alongside COTS parts (rail buttons, hardware, electronics) to produce a full rocket model. A BOM was created to estimate costs.

The most iterated component was the Electronics Bay (E-Bay). All iterations are shown below.

Bill of Materials used to estimate project cost.
V1 — designed to sit in the nose cone. The main concern was whether the threads could hold everything in place on descent.
V1 printed. Threading held well, so the next step was fitting all electronics inside.
V2 — houses all electronics: LiPo battery, screw switch, Blue Raven altimeter, Featherweight GPS, and a backup altimeter.
V2 exploded view including all screws and COTS components.
V3 — voltage calculations were wrong; needed 3 batteries and 3 screw switches. Space became too cramped.
V4 — switched to a standard dual-deploy E-Bay housing for slightly more working space.
Final design — more compact, better battery layout. Removed backup altimeter, reducing to two batteries and two screw switches.
Final exploded view with all COTS components.
Additional view of the final E-Bay design.
Final design printed and test fitted.

Outcome

I laser-cut and 3D-printed the remaining components of my rocket (including the fin drying supports) and it is awaiting final construction and launch.
