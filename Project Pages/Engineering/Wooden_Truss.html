Wooden Truss

Designing and creating a wooden truss with certain constraints to withstand a 1000lb force.

Overview & Role

The culmination of the Mechanics I course, this project tasked us with using the formulas we had learned to calculate forces and stresses in various truss designs, then applying those calculations and our general engineering.

This was a team project and everyone worked together on the mass majority of the project. In particular, I took lead in assembling the truss but worked less on the CAD.

Example finite element analysis results for the truss under the applied load.

Calculations

We took into account material properties, force distribution, and size constraints among other things to design a truss that could withstand the required load.

Example calculations for the forces on each member of the truss under the applied load.

Finite Element Analysis

We CADDed up our design and ran finite element analysis to verify that the calculations we made were indeed correct.

Finite element analysis results for the truss under the applied load.

Construction

We used the vertical band saw to cut the wooden members to size, then assembled the truss using wood glue, lasercut gusset plates, and clamps. We had to be careful to ensure that the angles were correct and that everything was held firmly in place while the glue dried.

The file we used to cut the gusset plates on the laser cutter.

Testing

We tested the truss by applying an increasing load to it and measuring the deflection. We reached failure earlier than expected due to gusset plate failure, a failure mode that we had not accounted for (it was outside the scope of the class).
